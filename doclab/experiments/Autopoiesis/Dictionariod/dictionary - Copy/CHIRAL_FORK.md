# Chiral Fork

## Canonical (Pirouette)
The Chiral Fork is the fundamental choice point within a Chiral Shift where a system, under critical stress, selects one of three topological solutions‚ÄîAchromatic, Left-Handed, or Right-Handed‚Äîto form a new, stable resonant pattern. This choice is governed by the Principle of Maximal Coherence, as the system adopts the topology offering the highest value for its Pirouette Lagrangian (`ùìõ_p`) under the new environmental conditions. It is the mechanism by which systems facing collapse find a new, coherent way to exist.

## EFT-First Summary
The Chiral Fork is conceptually equivalent to **Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking**, a core concept in quantum field theory and condensed matter physics. A system in a symmetric, high-energy state (e.g., above a critical temperature) will, upon "cooling" (i.e., resolving overwhelming stress), settle into a lower-energy ground state that breaks the original symmetry. The choice between Left-Handed and Right-Handed solutions is a direct analog to a system selecting a specific vacuum expectation value that breaks a continuous symmetry (like U(1)) or a discrete parity symmetry.
